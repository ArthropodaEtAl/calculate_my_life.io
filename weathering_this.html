Weathering This

Originally published in Colorado's Best Emerging Poets 2019 - Z Publishing House pg 72


After the storm

the air cools like our imprints in satin sheets.

the naked leaves glisten

raindrops, like sweat and sea spray from our

jogs down the pier

and the sun gleams

like our widened eyes reflecting the yellow glow

of a bedside lamp


I brought you

alone, us two,

to behold the view

wettened, shivering, tired

shining, and content

enchanted
