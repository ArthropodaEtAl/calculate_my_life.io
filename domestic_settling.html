Domestic Settling

Originally published in High Grade - V.2021 pg 12


Where the water winds there are groves

where lovers have been buried

clasped even in death to each other:

unnatural morbidity


As water, I know I shall flow, through predisposed beauty.

The deep crevases will accept new scars from ripples.

The exuberant sun shall make smooth surfaces shine

and the valley will be flooded with fauna-feeding flora,

making groves for lovers to stash their tombstones.


Shall it be beautiful?

I have no doubts.

Expected elegance, inherited time and time again


Unless I am not water.

I began knowing, but now I am not so sure

and then where would I go?

Would any other existence surmount predisposition?

So, I, a daughter of water, could only be water in turn.
